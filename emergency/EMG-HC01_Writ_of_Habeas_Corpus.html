WRIT OF HABEAS CORPUS AD SUBJICIENDUM

To: Any Officer, Custodian, or Public Servant detaining the body of the living man known as Gregory-Alan: Williams

From: Williams Family Estate™ – by next friend, agent, or Successor Trustee

Comes now the next friend of the living man known as Gregory-Alan: Williams, a free man upon the land, held in private trust, not a subject of any statutory jurisdiction nor surety for any legal fiction.

It is hereby demanded under the supreme law of the land and the protections guaranteed under the North Carolina Constitution, Article I, Section 21 and the Constitution for the United States of America, Article I, Section 9, Clause 2, that the body of the man be immediately brought forth with full cause shown as to any lawful detention, and that any and all claims to jurisdiction be proven with specificity and supporting evidence of injury or verified complaint by a living man or woman.

Let no officer deny this writ under color of statute, policy, or agency directive. The right to Habeas Corpus shall not be suspended unless in cases of rebellion or invasion, which are not present.
