This document functions as:

  • A trigger for judicial review via habeas corpus
  • A commercial conditional acceptance for value

  • Triggers production of jurisdictional authority, bond trail, and verified injury
  • Enters into lawful record and becomes standing truth in commerce if unrebutted

Who May Deliver

  • Successor trustee
  • Authorized agent (even verbally)

Best Practice Delivery Steps

  1. Print 2 copies: one for Clerk of Court, one for Sheriff or Jail Commander

  3. Deliver in person to Jail or Sheriff with clear statement of authority
  4. Keep a delivery log or affidavit of service as proof

Step-by-Step Filing Instructions

  1. Print and sign a fresh Affidavit of Service on the last page of this packet.
  2. Attach the notarized Writ of Habeas Corpus and make 2 copies.
  3. Take the packet to the county courthouse where he is being held.
  4. Go to the Clerk of Court's office or the Magistrate window.
  5. Say clearly and calmly:
    “I am delivering a lawful Writ of Habeas Corpus on behalf of the living man Gregory-Alan. I am his next friend. He is being held without verified claim or lawful jurisdiction. This is a jurisdictional demand. Please file this immediately.”
  6. Hand them:
    • The original notarized writ
    • The signed Affidavit of Service

  7. If they refuse to file it, ask for their full name and title, then record that in writing. Do not argue. Simply say:
    “This refusal will be recorded as a trespass upon a man's right to remedy under the Constitution and equity. I reserve all rights.”
  8. Optionally, also deliver a second certified copy to the Sheriff's Office if he is being held at the county jail.

Remain calm. You are not asking for a favor — you are invoking a lawful right. This is a demand for proof of jurisdiction, not a plea.
