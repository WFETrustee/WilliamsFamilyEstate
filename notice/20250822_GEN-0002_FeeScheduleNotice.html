Public Notice: Fee & Penalty Schedule for Dishonor and Injury to the Res

Filed: August 22, 2025

Venue: WilliamsFamilyEstate.org Public Notice Registry — Identifier: GEN-0002

This public notice publishes the official Fee & Penalty Schedule of the Williams Family Estate ™ (“Estate”). The schedule establishes liquidated damages applicable upon dishonor, breach, or injury in commerce or equity, including refusal of lawful tender, impairment of creditworthiness, levy or seizure, identity misuse, interference with beneficiaries, trespass, compelled medical procedures, wrongful death, censorship/suppression of notice, privacy violations, conversion, contempt of trust autonomy, and trustee self-dealing. All terms below are issued in honor and good conscience to preserve the res and protect beneficiaries.

1) Administrative Dishonors

  • Failure to respond within cure period to any lawful notice, affidavit, or tender: $10,000 per occurrence.
  • Failure or refusal to honor perfected Estate Performance Bond when tendered for settlement or discharge: $100,000 per occurrence.
  • Refusal of lawful tender (e.g., marking instruments “refused” or “frivolous” without lawful cause): $250,000 per occurrence.
  • Interference, delay, or obstruction of settlement or discharge tendered under bond: $50,000/day, per responsible party.
  • Damage to Estate creditworthiness (derogatory reporting, suspension of service, unlawful collection after tender): $500,000 per occurrence plus $10,000/day until remedy.
  • Contemptuous willful dishonor of perfected bond resulting in material harm to the Estate or beneficiaries: $5,000,000 per occurrence.

2) Direct Injuries to the Trust Res

  • Unlawful levy, lien, garnishment, seizure, or distraint of Estate property after tender of lawful remedy: $1,000,000 per occurrence plus $25,000/day until full restoration.
  • Compelled medical procedure (including forced vaccination, invasive testing, or unwanted treatment of any beneficiary without Trustee consent): $2,000,000 per occurrence plus $50,000/day until remedy.
  • Wrongful death of any beneficiary resulting from unlawful action, neglect, coercion, or violation of estate rights: $20,000,000 per occurrence, enforceable jointly and severally.
  • Unauthorized removal, detention, or interference with the care, custody, or moral upbringing of any beneficiary: $5,000,000 per occurrence.

3) Identity, Privacy, and Information Injuries

  • Unlawful use of Estate Name, Title, or identifying numbers (SSN, CUSIP, EIN, etc.) without Trustee authority: $500,000 per instance.
  • Compelled or presumed joinder of the Trustee into statutory personhood or public office without express written consent: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Tampering with, obstructing, withholding, or falsifying estate correspondence (physical or electronic): $250,000 per occurrence.
  • Suppression, censorship, alteration, or removal of any published Estate notice or lawful declaration: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Breach of privacy or unauthorized surveillance of Estate communications or activities: $500,000 per act.

4) Property and Spiritual Injuries

  • Unlawful search, seizure, or trespass upon lands, dwellings, or private property of the Estate without Trustee consent: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Conversion, theft, or misappropriation of Estate property or chattel: $1,000,000 per occurrence, plus restoration in full.
  • Deliberate infliction of psychological harm, coercion, indoctrination, or spiritual trespass upon beneficiaries contrary to Estate moral charter: $2,000,000 per occurrence.

5) Trustee Misconduct (Self‑Dealing)

  • Breach of fiduciary duty / self‑dealing: Any trustee, officer, or agent who conspires, contrives, or acts with intent to unjustly enrich the Estate through fraud, malicious inducement, or deliberate harm to another estate, trust, or man/woman, contrary to equity and good conscience: $10,000,000 per occurrence, plus immediate removal from office and personal liability for all damages caused.

Terms of Enforcement

All fees, penalties, and damages are immediately due and payable upon dishonor or injury. Failure to cure within ten (10) days of notice constitutes default and consent to enforcement. All sums are enforceable in equity and commerce as lawful obligations of the dishonoring or injuring party. Remedies include, without limitation: setoff, lien, levy, or commercial claim.

Publication

This schedule is hereby published as public notice and constitutes lawful terms of engagement in all dealings with the Williams Family Estate ™. This instrument is issued for education and notice; the Estate reserves all rights without recourse.
