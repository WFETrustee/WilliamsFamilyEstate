Public Notice: Schedule of Liquidated Damages for Dishonor and Injury to the Res

Filed: August 22, 2025

Venue: WilliamsFamilyEstate.org Public Notice Registry — Identifier: GEN-0001

This Notice publishes the official Schedule of Liquidated Damages of the Williams Family Estate™ (“Estate”). The amounts stated below are intended as fair pre‑estimates of loss to compensate injury to the res and to commerce. They are issued in equity, are compensatory and not punitive, and scale with the gravity and consequences of any dishonor or injury. All terms are offered in honor and good conscience.

For the avoidance of doubt, all sums set forth herein are declared as liquidated and compensatory damages, not penalties or forfeitures. They represent fair pre-estimates of loss, injury, or risk to the res and beneficiaries, calculated to restore the Estate to the position it ought to have occupied had no dishonor or injury occurred. This schedule is grounded in the maxims of equity, including that equity abhors a penalty and equity will not suffer a wrong without a remedy. The Estate does not seek punishment, only to be made whole in honor and good conscience.

1) Administrative Dishonors (Compensatory)

  • Non‑response within cure period to any lawful notice, affidavit, or tender: $500 per day of non‑response, capped at $10,000.
  • Failure or refusal to honor perfected Estate Performance Bond when tendered for settlement or discharge: $100,000 per occurrence.
  • Refusal of lawful tender (e.g., marking instruments “refused” or “frivolous” without lawful cause): $10,000 or demonstrable actual damages, whichever is greater.
  • Interference, delay, or obstruction that materially prevents or frustrates settlement under bond: $50,000 per day, per responsible party.
  • Damage to Estate creditworthiness (derogatory reporting, suspension of service, unlawful collection after tender): $500,000 per occurrence plus $10,000 per day until remedy, or demonstrable actual damages if greater.
  • Contemptuous, willful dishonor of perfected bond resulting in material harm to the Estate or beneficiaries: $5,000,000 per occurrence.

2) Direct Injuries to the Trust Res

  • Unlawful levy, lien, garnishment, seizure, or distraint of Estate property after tender of lawful remedy: $1,000,000 per occurrence plus $25,000 per day until full restoration.
  • Compelled medical procedure (including forced vaccination, invasive testing, or unwanted treatment of any beneficiary without Trustee consent): $2,000,000 per occurrence plus $50,000 per day until remedy.
  • Wrongful death of any beneficiary from unlawful action, neglect, coercion, or violation of Estate rights: $20,000,000 per occurrence, enforceable jointly and severally.
  • Unauthorized removal, detention, or interference with the care, custody, or moral upbringing of any beneficiary: $5,000,000 per occurrence.
  • False or malicious report to CPS or any child-protective, welfare, or similar agency resulting in investigation, removal, or interference with beneficiaries: $1,000,000 per act, or demonstrable actual damages if greater.
  • Presumption of jurisdiction or authority over any beneficiary without express Trustee consent (including ex parte administrative proceedings, emergency removals without immediate due notice, or coercive presumptions of wardship): $2,000,000 per occurrence plus $50,000 per day until remedy.
  • Failure to promptly return a beneficiary after wrongful removal, detention, or interference (whether by agency, contractor, or agent): $100,000 per day until full restoration to the Estate’s peace and custody.

3) Identity, Privacy, and Information Injuries

  • Unlawful use of Estate Name, Title, or identifying numbers (SSN, CUSIP, EIN, etc.) without Trustee authority: $500,000 per instance.
  • Compelled or presumed joinder of the Trustee into statutory personhood or public office without express written consent: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Tampering with, obstructing, withholding, or falsifying Estate correspondence (physical or electronic): $250,000 per occurrence.
  • Suppression, censorship, alteration, or removal of any published Estate notice or lawful declaration: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Breach of privacy or unauthorized surveillance of Estate communications or activities: $500,000 per act.

4) Property and Spiritual Injuries

  • Unlawful search, seizure, or trespass upon lands, dwellings, or private property of the Estate without Trustee consent: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Conversion, theft, or misappropriation of Estate property or chattel: $1,000,000 per occurrence, plus restoration in full.
  • Deliberate infliction of psychological harm, coercion, indoctrination, or spiritual trespass upon beneficiaries contrary to the Estate’s moral charter: $2,000,000 per occurrence.

5) Trustee Misconduct (Self‑Dealing)

  • Breach of fiduciary duty / self‑dealing: Any trustee, officer, or agent who conspires, contrives, omits, or acts with intent, willful neglect, or reckless disregard in order to unjustly enrich the Estate through fraud, malicious inducement, or deliberate harm to another estate, trust, or man/woman, contrary to equity and good conscience: $10,000,000 per occurrence, plus immediate removal from office and personal liability for all damages caused.

General Principle of Continuing Harm:

Where an injury or dishonor persists over time, liquidated damages shall accrue daily until remedy, whether or not daily accrual is expressly stated in the category above. Nothing herein limits the Estate’s right to prove and recover actual damages if greater than the liquidated sums declared.

Terms of Enforcement

All amounts are liquidated damages — fair pre‑estimates of loss — and are immediately due and payable upon dishonor or injury. Failure to cure within ten (10) days of notice constitutes default and consent to enforcement. All sums are enforceable in equity and commerce as lawful obligations of the dishonoring or injuring party. Remedies include, without limitation: setoff, lien, levy, or commercial claim. Nothing herein waives the Estate’s right to prove and recover actual damages if greater.

Publication

This schedule is hereby published as public notice and constitutes lawful terms of engagement in all dealings with the Estate. By entering into any commercial interaction, correspondence, or dealing with the Estate, all parties are deemed to have constructive notice of this Schedule of Liquidated Damages and consent to these terms of engagement.

This Notice of schedule is issued to preserve the res and protect beneficiaries; all rights reserved, without recourse.
