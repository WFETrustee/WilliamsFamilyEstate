Trust Fee Schedule:

Schedule of Liquidated Damages for Dishonor and Injury to the Res

Filed: August 22, 2025

Venue: WilliamsFamilyEstate.org Public Notice Registry

This Notice publishes the official Schedule of Liquidated Damages of the Williams Family Estate™ (“Estate”). The amounts stated below are intended as fair pre-estimates of loss to compensate injury to the res and to commerce. They are issued in equity, are compensatory and not punitive, and scale with the gravity and consequences of any dishonor or injury. All terms are offered in honor and good conscience.

For the avoidance of doubt, all sums set forth herein are declared as liquidated and compensatory damages, not penalties or forfeitures. They represent fair pre-estimates of loss, injury, or risk to the res and beneficiaries, calculated to restore the Estate to the position it ought to have occupied had no dishonor or injury occurred. This schedule is grounded in the maxims of equity, including that equity abhors a penalty and equity will not suffer a wrong without a remedy. The Estate does not seek punishment, only to be made whole in honor and good conscience.

All amounts herein are the minimum liquidated damages enforceable upon notice and presentment, and do not limit the Estate’s right to seek additional equitable compensation where actual loss exceeds the scheduled sums. Fees are cumulative and may accrue concurrently from the date of injury until cure, restoration, or final settlement.

1) Administrative Dishonors (Compensatory)

  • Non-response within cure period to any lawful notice, affidavit, or tender: $500 per day of non-response, capped at $10,000.
  • Failure or refusal to honor perfected Estate Performance Bond when tendered for settlement or discharge: $100,000 per occurrence.
  • Refusal of lawful tender (e.g., marking instruments “refused” or “frivolous” without lawful cause): $10,000 or demonstrable actual damages, whichever is greater.
  • Interference, delay, or obstruction that materially prevents or frustrates settlement under bond: $50,000 per day, per responsible party.
  • Damage to Estate creditworthiness (derogatory reporting, suspension of service, unlawful collection after tender): $500,000 per occurrence plus $10,000 per day until remedy, or demonstrable actual damages if greater.
  • Contemptuous, willful dishonor of perfected bond resulting in material harm to the Estate or beneficiaries: $2,000,000 per occurrence.

2) Direct Injuries to the Trust Res

  • Unlawful levy, lien, garnishment, seizure, or distraint of Estate property after tender of lawful remedy: $1,000,000 per occurrence plus $25,000 per day until full restoration.
  • Compelled medical procedure (including forced vaccination, invasive testing, or unwanted treatment of any beneficiary without Trustee consent): $2,000,000 per occurrence plus $50,000 per day until remedy.
  • Wrongful death of any beneficiary arising from unlawful act, neglect, coercion, or violation of Estate rights: $50,000,000 per occurrence as liquidated and compensatory damages, or demonstrable actual damages if greater, enforceable jointly and severally.
  • Unauthorized removal, detention, or interference with the care, custody, or moral upbringing of any beneficiary: $5,000,000 per occurrence.
  • False or malicious report to CPS or any child-protective, welfare, or similar agency resulting in investigation, removal, or interference with beneficiaries: $1,000,000 per act, or demonstrable actual damages if greater.
  • Presumption of jurisdiction or authority over any beneficiary without express Trustee consent (including ex parte administrative proceedings, emergency removals without immediate due notice, or coercive presumptions of wardship): $2,000,000 per occurrence plus $50,000 per day until remedy.
  • Failure to promptly return a beneficiary after wrongful removal, detention, or interference (whether by agency, contractor, or agent): $10,000 per hour until full restoration to the Estate’s peace and custody.

3) Identity, Privacy, and Information Injuries

  • Unlawful use of Estate Name, Title, or identifying numbers (SSN, CUSIP, EIN, etc.) without Trustee authority: $500,000 per instance.
  • Compelled or presumed joinder of the Trustee into statutory personhood or public office without express written consent: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Tampering with, obstructing, withholding, or falsifying Estate correspondence (physical or electronic): $250,000 per occurrence.
  • Suppression, censorship, alteration, or removal of any published Estate notice or lawful declaration: $1,000,000 per occurrence.
  • Breach of privacy or unauthorized surveillance of Estate communications or activities: $500,000 per act.

4) Property and Spiritual Injuries

  • Unlawful search, seizure, or trespass upon lands, dwellings, or private property of the Estate without Trustee consent: $500,000 per occurrence.
  • Conversion, theft, or wrongful detention of trust property (including any chattel or asset of the Estate): $1,000,000 per occurrence plus full restoration of the property or equivalent value, together with compensation for loss of use including (a) prevailing fair market hire of the property wrongfully taken or withheld, (b) short-term rental, lodging, or substitute costs reasonably incurred, and (c) related incidentals arising from deprivation of normal use, until restoration is complete.
  • Conversion or wrongful detention of a house of the Estate: $1,000,000 per occurrence plus full restoration of the property or equivalent value, together with compensation for loss of use, including hotel or lodging costs, meals, and other reasonable incidentals necessitated by deprivation of cooking and living facilities, until restoration is complete.
  • Deliberate infliction of psychological harm, coercion, indoctrination, or spiritual trespass upon beneficiaries contrary to the Estate’s moral charter: $2,000,000 per occurrence.

5) Trustee Misconduct (Self-Dealing)

  • Breach of fiduciary duty / self-dealing: Any trustee, officer, or agent who conspires, contrives, omits, or acts with intent, willful neglect, or reckless disregard in order to unjustly enrich the Estate through fraud, malicious inducement, or deliberate harm to another estate, trust, or man/woman, contrary to equity and good conscience: $10,000,000 per occurrence, plus immediate removal from office and personal liability for all damages caused.

Terms of Enforcement

Accrual of Damages and Continuing Harm

All sums herein accrue from the date of dishonor or injury until full cure, restoration, or settlement. Where an injury or dishonor persists over time, liquidated damages shall continue to accrue daily until remedy, whether or not daily accrual is expressly stated in the categories above. Interest at the prevailing commercial rate may be applied to unpaid balances as additional compensation for delay.

Severability

If any provision of this Schedule of Liquidated Damages is found unenforceable or invalid in any forum, all remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. Each clause stands independently as a lawful pre-estimate of loss, issued in equity to restore the Estate, and shall not be impaired by the invalidity of any other clause.

Non-Waiver

No delay, silence, omission, or partial exercise of rights by the Estate shall be deemed a waiver of any right, remedy, or claim. All rights remain preserved in equity and enforceable in full honor.

Cumulative Remedies

The remedies declared herein are cumulative. Assertion of one remedy shall not preclude pursuit of others in equity or commerce to secure full restoration of the res.

Constructive Notice

By correspondence, contract, or interaction with the Williams Family Estate™, all parties are deemed to have constructive notice of this Schedule and to have consented to its terms of engagement.

Survival

Obligations under this Schedule survive any settlement, discharge, or closure of the matter giving rise to the claim until full performance is rendered and the Estate is made whole.

Governing Jurisdiction (Equity)

All matters arising under this Schedule shall be heard in equity and conscience, under the maxims of equity and the private trust authority of the Williams Family Estate™.

Estoppel

Failure to rebut this Schedule point-for-point by sworn affidavit within the cure period constitutes admission, agreement, and permanent estoppel against denial. All parties with notice, actual or constructive, are barred in equity and conscience from later contesting its terms.

Publication

This Schedule is published as lawful notice and constitutes terms of engagement in all dealings with the Estate. All parties entering commerce or correspondence with the Estate are deemed to have constructive notice and to consent to these terms. Notice to any agent is notice to the principal, and notice to the principal is notice to all agents.

This Notice of schedule is issued to preserve the res and protect beneficiaries; all rights reserved, without recourse.
