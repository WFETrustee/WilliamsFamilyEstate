Notice of Trustee Capacity Clarification and Prospective Alignment

Published: December 21, 2025

By: Gregory-Alan Williams, Trustee
Williams Family Estate™

Notice of Clarification

This notice is published in good faith for the purpose of clarity, alignment, and prevention of misunderstanding.

The Trustee acknowledges that, in prior private dealings, there existed an incomplete understanding regarding when public clarification of trustee capacity is equitably required, particularly where conditional or anticipatory correspondence with third parties is contemplated.

The Trustee affirms that it has always been the intention to steward matters of substance—including real and intangible property—within the framework of private trust administration, while preserving the privacy of the trust res and the protection of beneficiaries, except where disclosure is equitably required to prevent confusion or injustice.

Prospective Clarification of Capacity

Accordingly, the Trustee hereby clarifies that any conditional, anticipatory, or responsive communications issued now or hereafter concerning matters touching the Estate are made in a trustee capacity, and not in a personal or individual capacity, unless expressly stated otherwise.

This clarification is made prospectively, prior to the receipt or reliance upon any pending conditional correspondence by third parties, and is not intended to alter, deny, or impair any lawful obligation, security interest, or reliance properly arising before such clarification.

No Retroactive Effect Asserted

This notice does not assert retroactive recharacterization of past representations, nor does it seek to defeat any reliance formed in good faith prior to this clarification.

Rather, it serves to align outward notice with matured understanding, so that all future interactions may proceed with transparency of capacity and fairness of expectation.

Preservation of Privacy

Consistent with long-standing trust practice, the specific contents of the trust res remain private and are not disclosed herein. This notice is limited to the clarification of capacity and does not enumerate assets, beneficiaries, or internal trust accounting.

Statement of Good Faith

This notice is issued in the spirit of equity, conscience, and prevention of confusion. It is not adversarial in nature, nor is it intended as a declaration of dispute. It is published solely to ensure that no party may be misled as to the capacity in which the Trustee acts in matters now or hereafter touching the Estate.

So published, this 21st day of December, 2025.

Gregory-Alan Williams
Trustee, Williams Family Estate™
