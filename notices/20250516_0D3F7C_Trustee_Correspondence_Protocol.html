Standing Notice of Trustee Correspondence Protocol

Filed: May 16, 2025

Venue: Williams Family Estate™ – Public Notice Record  |  Identifier: 0D3F7C

To All Men and Women, Agents, Entities, or Public Officials:

Be it known by this permanent public notice that the Trustee of the Williams Family Estate™, a private trust established by divine right and private contract, has adopted the following standing correspondence protocol governing all notices, communications, and presentments received by post or digital transmission.

1. Mailing Address and Monitoring

The Trust maintains a distinct mailing venue separate from the domicile of the living man. All estate-related correspondence shall be sent to the Trust’s published mailing location. This address is monitored and reviewed weekly or more frequently, as capacity permits. To preserve the estate's private nature and honor, correspondence sent to the living man directly may be deemed misdirected or held in abeyance.

2. Response Timeframe

All notices and correspondence received by the Trustee are subject to a thirty (30) day rebuttal preservation window, even where customary commercial timeframes suggest shorter periods (e.g., 3-day estoppel or 10-day reply). No dishonor shall be presumed by silence unless express notice and opportunity to cure are documented in accordance with maxims of equity and proper jurisdiction.

3. Sabbatical, Rest, or Absence

During periods of spiritual sabbatical, family retreat, medical leave, or declared rest, notice review and reply may be suspended. However, all rights and rebuttals are preserved and may be asserted retroactively within a lawful time once the Trustee resumes administrative duties.

4. Witness and Clerk Delegation

At the Trustee’s discretion, an authorized witness clerk may log, scan, or digitally deliver incoming correspondence for review. Such delegation shall not be construed as waiver of responsibility or transfer of authority, but rather a ministerial act to preserve honor and timely diligence.

5. Electronic Correspondence

Where offered, electronic mail or secure upload systems may be used to supplement, but not replace, physical presentment unless explicitly agreed upon. Any such email shall include sender’s lawful name, return address, and a clear indication of intent and capacity.

6. Reservation of Rights

The Trustee reserves all rights, remedies, and immunities under natural law, equity, and divine jurisdiction. No implied contract, agreement, or dishonor shall be assumed from procedural delay absent intentional and knowing default.

This Notice shall remain in force until replaced by a superseding publication, duly filed in the public record of the Estate.
